## 📘 What DeepPaperNote does

DeepPaperNote helps you turn dense research papers into notes you can reuse. It is built for Obsidian and works well with paper reading, literature notes, and Zotero-based research.

Use it when you want to:

- save key ideas from academic papers
- create clean Markdown notes
- keep your notes ready for Obsidian
- read papers with less friction
- build a literature system you can use again

## 🖥️ What you need

DeepPaperNote is made for Windows users who want a simple setup.

You need:

- Windows 10 or Windows 11
- a stable internet connection for the first download
- enough disk space for your notes and paper files
- Obsidian if you want to open the notes there
- Zotero if you keep your papers in a paper library

If you store a lot of papers, keep your files in one folder so they are easy to find.

## ✍️ How to use it

DeepPaperNote follows a simple flow.

1. Open the app.
2. Add or select a research paper.
3. Let the app read the paper content.
4. Review the generated deep-reading notes.
5. Save the notes as Markdown.
6. Open the note in Obsidian.

The output is meant to be easy to edit. You can keep the note as-is or add your own comments.

## 🧾 What the notes contain

The notes are built for deep reading and reuse. They may include:

- paper title
- author names
- research question
- main claims

- key terms
- important findings
- quotes or excerpts
- your own reading notes
- links back to the paper source

The note format is clean and works well with Markdown. That makes it simple to store in Obsidian and easy to search later.
